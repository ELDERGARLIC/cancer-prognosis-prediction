### Interpretation

1. **C-index jumped dramatically (+0.26)**. This is the expected payoff of the new Cox partial-likelihood + ordinal heads — the model is now learning a *survival-aware* risk score rather than a pure multinomial classifier. `c_index_cls = 0.632` means ordering by predicted class probabilities already gives good concordance.
2. **Classification accuracy / AUC-ROC regressed** across both the GAT and the hybrid RF. Several likely drivers:
   - Optimisation budget is now split across 3 loss heads (CE + Cox + Ordinal). The main CE objective gets less gradient pressure (`aux=0.3 · 0 (off) + cox=0.5 + ord=0.2`). Visible in the log: `main≈1.40` barely moves while `cox≈2.2–2.4` dominates the train loss.
   - Early-stopping on AUC triggers after ~35–60 epochs vs 100+ previously, so the CE head never fully converges.
   - Tumour-stage feature was in the previous Apr 14 run (6 clinical features) but collapses to all-NaN here (4 features), removing a known-useful prognostic signal.
   - 600 genes with 34% isolation vs 200 genes with 81% isolation — the KG is denser but the per-sample feature dim tripled, and class-balanced learning on a fixed 956-sample training fold is harder.
3. **Fold variance shrank** (AUC std 0.063 → 0.016). The AUC-based early-stopping + larger patience produced much more consistent folds, even though the mean dropped.
4. **Cox PH baseline is unchanged (0.748)**. It still dominates on C-index, now by only ~0.11 over our GAT (was 0.37). The gap to the Cox ceiling has shrunk substantially.
5. **Explainability shifted**. Apr 14 top genes (CCDC117, TM9SF2, GPR152, NCKAP5, …) overlap only on `CCDC117` with the Apr 19 top-10 (`FGD6, GUCY1B2, NAB1, PCDHGA6, IMMP1L, TTLL2, FCHSD1, DAND5, CCDC117, TUG1`). Different LASSO draws over the larger 600-gene pool dominate GNNExplainer's rankings. SHAP continues to rank age (`clinical_0`) at or near the top.

### Was the change a win?

Mixed, but survival-aware:

- ✅ **If the downstream use-case is risk ranking** (which is the clinical reality for prognosis), **C-index 0.636 vs 0.380 is a major win** — more than half the gap to the Cox ceiling was closed.
- ⚠️ **If the headline metric is classification accuracy**, the Apr 14 Calibrated RF (0.454) still beats this run (0.421). That regression is ~3 pp and within ±1 SD of fold noise.
- ➖ The drop in GCN / RF baselines (all dropped by 3–4 pp in the same direction) strongly suggests a **data-side cause** (stage feature loss + 3× flattened feature dim → harder fitting) rather than a GAT-specific one.

### Recommended follow-ups

1. Recover `tumor_stage` by pulling the pathologic stage column from the GDC clinical JSON (not just the cached TSV) or mapping from `ajcc_pathologic_tumor_stage` when present.
2. Re-balance loss weights: try `cox_loss_weight 0.25`, `ordinal_loss_weight 0.1` to stop the Cox head from swamping CE optimization.
3. Decouple early-stopping: monitor a composite like `0.5 * c_index + 0.5 * auc_roc` to avoid stopping while CE accuracy is still improving.
4. Investigate the GNNExplainer `int64 overflow` for sample 5 (likely a node index out of range after graph batching).
5. Re-run GDC expression download with a retry/backoff to eliminate the 500 failure path.
